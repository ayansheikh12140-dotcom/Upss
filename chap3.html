Chapter 3: Periodic Table

Comprehensive Notes, Questions, and Practice for Sindh Textbook Board

Chapter Notes

This chapter discusses the development and structure of the Periodic Table, including Mendeleev's table, modern periodic table, groups, periods, and properties.

Mendeleev's Periodic Table

Dmitri Mendeleev arranged elements by atomic mass, leaving gaps for undiscovered elements. Properties repeat periodically.

Modern Periodic Table

Arranged by atomic number. Elements with similar properties in the same group (vertical) and period (horizontal).

Groups and Periods

  • Groups: Vertical columns (1-18), elements have similar chemical properties.
  • Periods: Horizontal rows (1-7), show gradual change in properties.

Metals, Non-Metals, and Metalloids

Metals: Left side, conduct electricity. Non-metals: Right side, poor conductors. Metalloids: Between, e.g., Silicon.

Valency and Atomic Size

Valency: Combining capacity. Atomic size decreases across a period, increases down a group.

Multiple Choice Questions (MCQs)

1. Who proposed the modern periodic table?

a) Mendeleev
b) Moseley
c) Bohr
d) Rutherford

Answer: b) Moseley

2. Elements in the same group have:

a) Same atomic mass
b) Same number of valence electrons
c) Same atomic number
d) Same mass number

Answer: b) Same number of valence electrons

3. The number of periods in the periodic table is:

a) 7
b) 8
c) 18
d) 9

Answer: a) 7

4. Which is a metalloid?

a) Sodium
b) Silicon
c) Sulfur
d) Chlorine

Answer: b) Silicon

5. Atomic size increases down a:

a) Period
b) Group
c) Neither
d) Both

Answer: b) Group

Short Questions

  1. Define periodic table.
    Answer: A table that arranges elements in order of increasing atomic number with similar properties in columns.
  2. Who is Mendeleev?
    Answer: Russian chemist who arranged elements by atomic mass and predicted new elements.
  3. What is a group?
    Answer: A vertical column in the periodic table with elements having similar properties.
  4. What is a period?
    Answer: A horizontal row in the periodic table showing gradual change in properties.
  5. Name the groups of the periodic table.
    Answer: 18 groups, named as IA to VIIIA, and transition metals.
  6. What are metals?
    Answer: Elements that are good conductors of heat and electricity, malleable, and ductile.
  7. What are non-metals?
    Answer: Elements that are poor conductors, brittle, and form acidic oxides.
  8. Define valency.
    Answer: The combining capacity of an element, determined by valence electrons.
  9. What is atomic size?
    Answer: The distance from the nucleus to the outermost electron shell.
  10. Give an example of a metalloid.
    Answer: Silicon or Germanium.

Long Questions

  1. Explain Mendeleev's periodic law.
    Answer: Properties of elements are periodic functions of their atomic masses.
  2. Describe the modern periodic table.
    Answer: Arranged by atomic number, 18 groups, 7 periods, metals on left, non-metals on right.
  3. Differentiate between metals and non-metals.
    Answer: Metals conduct electricity, non-metals do not; metals are malleable, non-metals are brittle.
  4. Discuss the trends in atomic size in groups and periods.
    Answer: Increases down a group, decreases across a period.
  5. What are the merits of the periodic table?
    Answer: Predicts properties, classifies elements, helps in understanding chemical behavior.

Chapter 3: Algebraic Expressions and Formulas

Comprehensive Notes, Questions, and Practice for Sindh Textbook Board

Chapter Notes

This chapter covers algebraic expressions, their types, operations, and important algebraic formulas.

Algebraic Expressions

Definition

An algebraic expression is a combination of constants, variables, and algebraic operations (+, -, ×, ÷).

Components of Algebraic Expressions
  • Constant: Fixed numerical value (e.g., 5, -3, 2.7)
  • Variable: Symbol representing unknown value (e.g., x, y, z)
  • Term: Product of constants and variables (e.g., 3x, -2y², 5)
  • Coefficient: Numerical factor of a term (e.g., in 3x, 3 is coefficient)
  • Like Terms: Terms with same variables and powers
  • Unlike Terms: Terms with different variables or powers

Types of Algebraic Expressions

  • Monomial: Expression with one term (e.g., 3x, 5y²)
  • Binomial: Expression with two terms (e.g., 2x + 3, x² - 4)
  • Trinomial: Expression with three terms (e.g., x² + 2x + 1)
  • Polynomial: Expression with one or more terms

Degree of Polynomials

The highest power of the variable in a polynomial is called its degree.

Examples

3x² + 2x + 1 → Degree: 2 (quadratic)

4x³ - 2x² + x - 5 → Degree: 3 (cubic)

7x + 3 → Degree: 1 (linear)

Operations on Algebraic Expressions

Addition and Subtraction

Combine like terms by adding/subtracting their coefficients.

Multiplication

Multiply each term of first expression with each term of second expression.

Division

Divide each term of numerator by the denominator.

Important Algebraic Formulas

Square Formulas
  • (a + b)² = a² + 2ab + b²
  • (a - b)² = a² - 2ab + b²
  • a² - b² = (a + b)(a - b)
Cube Formulas
  • (a + b)³ = a³ + 3a²b + 3ab² + b³
  • (a - b)³ = a³ - 3a²b + 3ab² - b³
  • a³ + b³ = (a + b)(a² - ab + b²)
  • a³ - b³ = (a - b)(a² + ab + b²)
Other Important Formulas
  • (a + b + c)² = a² + b² + c² + 2ab + 2bc + 2ca
  • a³ + b³ + c³ - 3abc = (a + b + c)(a² + b² + c² - ab - bc - ca)

Factorization

Factorization is the process of expressing an algebraic expression as a product of its factors.

Factorization Methods
  • Taking common factors
  • Grouping terms
  • Using algebraic identities
  • Splitting the middle term (for quadratic expressions)

Rational Expressions

A rational expression is an algebraic expression that can be written as the quotient of two polynomials.

Examples

(x² + 3x + 2)/(x + 1)

(2x - 1)/(x² - 4)

Multiple Choice Questions (MCQs)

1. What is the degree of the polynomial 3x² + 2x³ - 5x + 7?

a) 1
b) 2
c) 3
d) 4

Answer: c) 3 (highest power is 3)

2. Which of the following is a binomial?

a) 3x
b) 2x + 5
c) x² + 2x + 1
d) 4x³

Answer: b) 2x + 5 (two terms)

3. What is the expanded form of (x + 3)²?

a) x² + 9
b) x² + 3x + 9
c) x² + 6x + 9
d) x² + 9x + 9

Answer: c) x² + 6x + 9 (using (a+b)² = a² + 2ab + b²)

4. Factorize: x² - 9

a) (x-3)²
b) (x+3)²
c) (x-3)(x+3)
d) (x-9)(x+1)

Answer: c) (x-3)(x+3) (using a² - b² = (a-b)(a+b))

5. What is the coefficient of x² in 3x³ - 2x² + 5x - 1?

a) 3
b) -2
c) 5
d) -1

Answer: b) -2

6. Simplify: (2x + 3)(x - 4)

a) 2x² - 5x - 12
b) 2x² + 5x - 12
c) 2x² - 11x - 12
d) 2x² - 8x - 12

Answer: a) 2x² - 5x - 12 (2x×x + 2x×(-4) + 3×x + 3×(-4))

7. Which is a rational expression?

a) √x + 2
b) (x²-4)/(x-2)
c) 3x + 2y
d) x² + y²

Answer: b) (x²-4)/(x-2) (quotient of two polynomials)

8. What is (a+b)³ equal to?

a) a³ + b³
b) a³ + 3a²b + 3ab² + b³
c) a³ - 3a²b + 3ab² - b³
d) a³ + b³ + 3ab(a+b)

Answer: b) a³ + 3a²b + 3ab² + b³

9. Which terms are like terms?

a) 2x and 2y
b) 3x² and 2x
c) 5xy and 2yx
d) 4x and 4

Answer: c) 5xy and 2yx (same variables)

10. Factorize: x² + 5x + 6

a) (x+1)(x+6)
b) (x+2)(x+3)
c) (x-2)(x-3)
d) (x+2)(x+4)

Answer: b) (x+2)(x+3) (since 2+3=5 and 2×3=6)

Short Questions

  1. Define algebraic expression.
    Answer: Combination of constants, variables, and algebraic operations.
  2. Differentiate between like and unlike terms.
    Answer: Like terms have same variables and powers, unlike terms have different variables or powers.
  3. What is the degree of polynomial 4x³ - 2x + 7?
    Answer: 3 (highest power of x).
  4. State the formula for (a-b)².
    Answer: a² - 2ab + b².
  5. Factorize: x² - 16
    Answer: (x-4)(x+4) using a² - b² formula.
  6. What is a binomial? Give example.
    Answer: Expression with two terms, e.g., 2x + 3.
  7. Simplify: 3x + 2y - x + 5y
    Answer: 2x + 7y (combining like terms).
  8. Expand: (2x + 1)²
    Answer: 4x² + 4x + 1.
  9. What is factorization?
    Answer: Expressing an expression as product of its factors.
  10. Find product: (x+2)(x-2)
    Answer: x² - 4 (using a² - b² formula).

Long Questions

  1. Explain different types of algebraic expressions with examples.
    Answer: Monomial (3x, -2y²), Binomial (2x+3, a²-b), Trinomial (x²+2x+1), Polynomial (any expression with one or more terms). Each type is classified based on number of terms.
  2. Describe the process of factorization with examples.
    Answer: Factorization methods include: 1) Taking common factor (3x+6=3(x+2)), 2) Using identities (x²-9=(x-3)(x+3)), 3) Grouping (ax+ay+bx+by=a(x+y)+b(x+y)=(a+b)(x+y)), 4) Splitting middle term (x²+5x+6=x²+2x+3x+6=x(x+2)+3(x+2)=(x+2)(x+3)).
  3. Prove the algebraic identity (a+b)² = a² + 2ab + b² geometrically.
    Answer: Consider a square of side (a+b). Its area = (a+b)². This square can be divided into: one square of area a², one square of area b², and two rectangles each of area ab. Total area = a² + b² + ab + ab = a² + 2ab + b². Hence proved.
  4. Explain operations on algebraic expressions with examples.
    Answer: Addition: (3x+2)+(2x-1)=5x+1 (combine like terms). Subtraction: (5x+3)-(2x-1)=3x+4. Multiplication: (x+2)(x+3)=x²+3x+2x+6=x²+5x+6. Division: (6x²+9x)÷3x=2x+3 (divide each term).
  5. Discuss the importance and applications of algebraic formulas.
    Answer: Algebraic formulas simplify calculations, help in factorization, solve equations quickly, and have applications in geometry (area, volume formulas), physics (kinematic equations), and real-life problems (compound interest, growth calculations).

1. Add: 3x² + 2x - 1 and 2x² - 3x + 4

Solution: (3x² + 2x - 1) + (2x² - 3x + 4) = 3x² + 2x² + 2x - 3x - 1 + 4 = 5x² - x + 3

2. Multiply: (2x + 3)(x - 4)

Solution: (2x + 3)(x - 4) = 2x(x) + 2x(-4) + 3(x) + 3(-4) = 2x² - 8x + 3x - 12 = 2x² - 5x - 12

3. Expand: (3x - 2)²

Solution: Using (a-b)² = a² - 2ab + b², (3x - 2)² = (3x)² - 2(3x)(2) + (2)² = 9x² - 12x + 4

4. Factorize: x² + 7x + 12

Solution: We need two numbers that multiply to 12 and add to 7. Numbers are 3 and 4. So x² + 7x + 12 = (x + 3)(x + 4)

5. Simplify: (x² - 9)/(x - 3)

Solution: (x² - 9)/(x - 3) = [(x-3)(x+3)]/(x-3) = x + 3 (for x ≠ 3)

6. Find product using formula: 103 × 97

Solution: 103 × 97 = (100+3)(100-3) = 100² - 3² = 10000 - 9 = 9991

7. Factorize: 4x² - 25

Solution: 4x² - 25 = (2x)² - (5)² = (2x - 5)(2x + 5) using a² - b² formula

8. Expand: (2x + 3y)³

Solution: Using (a+b)³ = a³ + 3a²b + 3ab² + b³, (2x + 3y)³ = (2x)³ + 3(2x)²(3y) + 3(2x)(3y)² + (3y)³ = 8x³ + 36x²y + 54xy² + 27y³

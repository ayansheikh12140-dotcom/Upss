Chapter 2: Atomic Structure

Comprehensive Notes, Questions, and Practice for Sindh Textbook Board

Chapter Notes

This chapter explores the structure of atoms, including subatomic particles, atomic models, and related concepts.

Subatomic Particles

  • Electron: Negatively charged particle discovered by J.J. Thomson.
  • Proton: Positively charged particle in the nucleus, discovered by Rutherford.
  • Neutron: Neutral particle in the nucleus, discovered by Chadwick.

Atomic Models

  • Thomson's Model: Plum pudding model, electrons embedded in positive sphere.
  • Rutherford's Model: Nuclear model, dense nucleus with orbiting electrons.
  • Bohr's Model: Electrons in fixed orbits around the nucleus.

Atomic Number and Mass Number

Atomic number (Z) = number of protons. Mass number (A) = protons + neutrons. Isotopes have same Z but different A.

Electronic Configuration

Arrangement of electrons in shells: K (2), L (8), M (18), etc.

Multiple Choice Questions (MCQs)

1. Who discovered the electron?

a) Rutherford
b) Thomson
c) Bohr
d) Chadwick

Answer: b) Thomson

2. The nucleus contains:

a) Electrons
b) Protons and neutrons
c) Only neutrons
d) Only protons

Answer: b) Protons and neutrons

3. Isotopes have the same:

a) Mass number
b) Atomic number
c) Number of neutrons
d) Number of electrons

Answer: b) Atomic number

4. Bohr's model describes electrons in:

a) Random motion
b) Fixed orbits
c) Waves
d) Clouds

Answer: b) Fixed orbits

5. The charge of a proton is:

a) -1
b) 0
c) +1
d) +2

Answer: c) +1

Short Questions

  1. Define atomic number.
    Answer: The number of protons in the nucleus of an atom.
  2. What is an isotope?
    Answer: Atoms of the same element with the same atomic number but different mass numbers.
  3. Name the subatomic particles.
    Answer: Electron, proton, neutron.
  4. Who proposed the nuclear model?
    Answer: Ernest Rutherford.
  5. What is the charge of a neutron?
    Answer: Zero (neutral).
  6. Define mass number.
    Answer: The total number of protons and neutrons in an atom.
  7. What is electronic configuration?
    Answer: The arrangement of electrons in energy levels or shells.
  8. Who discovered the neutron?
    Answer: James Chadwick.
  9. What is Bohr's contribution?
    Answer: Proposed that electrons orbit the nucleus in fixed paths.
  10. Differentiate between proton and neutron.
    Answer: Proton has +1 charge, neutron has 0 charge.

Long Questions

  1. Describe Rutherford's gold foil experiment and its conclusions.
    Answer: Alpha particles were fired at gold foil. Most passed through, some deflected, indicating a dense nucleus.
  2. Explain the Bohr's atomic model.
    Answer: Electrons revolve in fixed orbits around the nucleus, absorbing or emitting energy in quanta.
  3. Discuss the discovery of subatomic particles.
    Answer: Electron by Thomson (cathode rays), proton by Rutherford (canal rays), neutron by Chadwick (nuclear reactions).
  4. What are isotopes? Give examples.
    Answer: Isotopes have same protons but different neutrons, e.g., Carbon-12 and Carbon-14.
  5. Explain electronic configuration with examples.
    Answer: Electrons fill shells: e.g., Hydrogen (1), Helium (2), Lithium (2,1).

1. An atom has 6 protons and 6 neutrons. Find its atomic number and mass number.

Solution: Atomic number = 6 (protons). Mass number = 6 + 6 = 12.

2. Calculate the number of neutrons in an atom with mass number 16 and atomic number 8.

Solution: Neutrons = 16 - 8 = 8.

3. Write the electronic configuration of oxygen (atomic number 8).

Solution: K=2, L=6.

4. An isotope of carbon has 8 neutrons. Find its mass number.

Solution: Carbon has 6 protons, so mass number = 6 + 8 = 14.

5. Find the number of protons, electrons, and neutrons in Na-23.

Solution: Protons=11, Electrons=11, Neutrons=12.

6. What is the atomic number of an element with 10 electrons?

Solution: Atomic number = 10 (Neon).

7. Calculate neutrons in Cl-37 (atomic number 17).

Solution: Neutrons = 37 - 17 = 20.

9. Find the electronic configuration of magnesium (atomic number 12).

Solution: K=2, L=8, M=2.

Solution: Aluminum, mass number 27.

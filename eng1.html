Chapter 1: The Last Sermon of Holy Prophet (PBUH)

A Study Guide on Ethics, Equality, and Human Rights

Chapter Notes üìñ

This chapter is a foundational text discussing the principles of **justice, equality, and human rights** as delivered by the Holy Prophet Muhammad (PBUH) in his final sermon.

Context of the Sermon

  • Date: 9th Dhu al-Hijjah, 10 A.H. (632 AD).
  • Occasion: During the Hajj (Pilgrimage).
  • Location: **Mount Arafat**, near Mecca.
  • Audience: Over **100,000 pilgrims** (Sermon of Farewell).

Key Teachings and Principles

The sermon is a comprehensive charter for humanity, covering:

  • **Sacredness of Life and Property:** Every Muslim's life and property is sacred and inviolable.
  • **Abolition of Interest (Riba):** All claims from the days of ignorance, especially interest, were abolished.
  • **Gender Rights:** Emphasis on the rights of women; they must be treated with kindness and respect.
  • **Equality of Mankind:** No superiority of Arab over non-Arab, or white over black, except by piety (**Taqwa**).
  • **Duty to Convey the Message:** The Prophet (PBUH) instructed the attendees to convey his message to those who were absent.
  • **Guidance:** Adherence to the **Quran** and the **Sunnah** (Prophet's practices).

1. The Last Sermon was delivered on:

a) Mount Hira
b) Mount Uhud
c) Mount Arafat
d) The Kaaba

Answer: c) Mount Arafat

2. In the Sermon, the Holy Prophet (PBUH) abolished the practice of:

a) Fasting
b) Riba (Interest)
c) Zakat
d) Trade

Answer: b) Riba (Interest)

3. According to the Sermon, superiority of one person over another is based on:

a) Wealth
b) Race
c) Piety (Taqwa)
d) Tribe

Answer: c) Piety (Taqwa)

4. The two things left behind for the followers, according to the Sermon, are the Quran and:

a) The Masjid
b) The Sunnah
c) The Caliphs
d) The wealth

Answer: b) The Sunnah

Short Questions ‚ùì

  1. Where and when was the Last Sermon delivered?
    Answer: It was delivered at Mount Arafat in the 10th year of Hijra (632 AD) during the Farewell Pilgrimage.
  2. What is the core message of the Sermon regarding equality?
    Answer: The Sermon emphasized that all human beings are equal; there is no superiority of an Arab over a non-Arab or vice versa, except on the basis of piety (Taqwa).
  3. What instructions were given about the rights of women?
    Answer: The Prophet (PBUH) commanded his followers to treat their women with kindness, justice, and love, and to remember their reciprocal rights and duties.
  4. What did the Holy Prophet (PBUH) abolish in the Sermon?
    Answer: He abolished all claims of vengeance, bloodshed, and usury/interest (**Riba**) from the days of ignorance.
  5. What should Muslims adhere to for guidance?
    Answer: They should adhere firmly to the **Book of Allah (Quran)** and the **Sunnah** (practices) of the Prophet (PBUH).

Long Questions üìù

  1. Discuss the importance of the Last Sermon as a charter of human rights.
    Answer: The Sermon is considered the first universal declaration of human rights. It guaranteed the right to life, property, and honor for all. It established gender rights, economic justice by abolishing interest, and racial equality, stating that piety is the only measure of superiority, long before modern human rights declarations.
  2. Explain the concept of equality taught in the Last Sermon.
    Answer: The sermon destroyed all notions of superiority based on race, color, or lineage. The Prophet (PBUH) clearly stated, "All mankind is from Adam and Eve." The standard for a person's worth is solely their **Taqwa** (fear of God/piety), establishing a truly egalitarian society.
  3. What responsibility did the Holy Prophet (PBUH) entrust to his followers at the end of the Sermon?
    Answer: He instructed those who were present to transmit his message to those who were absent, ensuring the principles of Islam, as outlined in the Sermon, were disseminated far and wide across the world for future generations.

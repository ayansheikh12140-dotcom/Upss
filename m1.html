Chapter 1: Real and Complex Numbers

Comprehensive Notes, Questions, and Practice for Sindh Textbook Board

Chapter Notes

This chapter covers real numbers, complex numbers, and their properties with applications in mathematics.

Real Numbers

Definition

Real numbers include all rational and irrational numbers that can be represented on the number line.

Types of Real Numbers
  • Natural Numbers (N): Counting numbers {1, 2, 3, ...}
  • Whole Numbers (W): Natural numbers including 0 {0, 1, 2, 3, ...}
  • Integers (Z): Whole numbers and their negatives {..., -3, -2, -1, 0, 1, 2, 3, ...}
  • Rational Numbers (Q): Numbers that can be expressed as p/q where p,q ∈ Z and q ≠ 0
  • Irrational Numbers: Numbers that cannot be expressed as p/q (e.g., √2, π)
Examples

Rational: 2/3, -5, 0.75, 0.333... (1/3)

Irrational: √2, π, e, √3

Properties of Real Numbers

  • Commutative Property: a + b = b + a; a × b = b × a
  • Associative Property: (a + b) + c = a + (b + c); (a × b) × c = a × (b × c)
  • Distributive Property: a × (b + c) = a × b + a × c
  • Additive Identity: a + 0 = a
  • Multiplicative Identity: a × 1 = a
  • Additive Inverse: a + (-a) = 0
  • Multiplicative Inverse: a × (1/a) = 1 (a ≠ 0)

Complex Numbers

Definition

A complex number is expressed in the form a + bi, where:
a = real part, b = imaginary part, i = √(-1) where i² = -1

Operations with Complex Numbers
  • Addition: (a + bi) + (c + di) = (a + c) + (b + d)i
  • Subtraction: (a + bi) - (c + di) = (a - c) + (b - d)i
  • Multiplication: (a + bi)(c + di) = (ac - bd) + (ad + bc)i
  • Division: (a + bi)/(c + di) = [(a + bi)(c - di)]/(c² + d²)
  • Conjugate: Conjugate of (a + bi) is (a - bi)
Complex Number Examples

Example 1: (3 + 2i) + (1 + 4i) = 4 + 6i

Example 2: (5 + 3i)(2 + i) = 10 + 5i + 6i + 3i² = 10 + 11i - 3 = 7 + 11i

Example 3: Conjugate of (4 - 7i) is (4 + 7i)

Number Line Representation

  • Real numbers can be represented on a straight horizontal line
  • Complex numbers are represented on the Argand plane (real axis and imaginary axis)

Short Questions

  1. Define rational numbers with examples.
    Answer: Numbers that can be expressed as p/q where p and q are integers and q ≠ 0. Examples: 2/3, -5, 0.75.
  2. What are irrational numbers? Give two examples.
    Answer: Numbers that cannot be expressed as p/q. Examples: √2, π.
  3. State the commutative property of addition.
    Answer: a + b = b + a for any real numbers a and b.
  4. What is a complex number?
    Answer: A number of the form a + bi where a and b are real numbers and i = √(-1).
  5. Find the conjugate of 7 - 4i.
    Answer: 7 + 4i.
  6. What is the additive identity of real numbers?
    Answer: 0, because a + 0 = a for any real number a.
  7. Simplify: (2 + 3i) + (4 - 5i)
    Answer: 6 - 2i.
  8. Differentiate between real and complex numbers.
    Answer: Real numbers can be represented on a number line, while complex numbers require a plane with real and imaginary axes.
  9. What is the multiplicative inverse of 5?
    Answer: 1/5.
  10. Is zero a rational number? Justify.
    Answer: Yes, because it can be expressed as 0/1, 0/2, etc.

Long Questions

  1. Explain the different types of real numbers with examples for each.
    Answer: Natural numbers (1,2,3...), Whole numbers (0,1,2,3...), Integers (...,-2,-1,0,1,2...), Rational numbers (2/3, -4, 0.5), Irrational numbers (√2, π). Real numbers include all these sets.
  2. Describe the properties of real numbers with examples.
    Answer: Commutative (3+5=5+3), Associative (2+(3+4)=(2+3)+4), Distributive (2×(3+4)=2×3+2×4), Identity properties (7+0=7, 9×1=9), Inverse properties (5+(-5)=0, 4×(1/4)=1).
  3. Explain complex numbers and their operations in detail.
    Answer: Complex numbers are of form a+bi. Addition: (a+bi)+(c+di)=(a+c)+(b+d)i. Subtraction: (a+bi)-(c+di)=(a-c)+(b-d)i. Multiplication: (a+bi)(c+di)=(ac-bd)+(ad+bc)i. Division uses conjugates to rationalize denominator.
  4. Prove that the sum of a complex number and its conjugate is a real number.
    Answer: Let z = a+bi, then conjugate ž = a-bi. Sum = (a+bi)+(a-bi) = 2a, which is a real number since it has no imaginary part.
  5. Discuss the representation of numbers on the number line and Argand diagram.
    Answer: Real numbers are represented on a horizontal number line. Complex numbers are represented on the Argand plane where x-axis represents real part and y-axis represents imaginary part. Point (a,b) represents complex number a+bi.

Practice Exercises

1. Simplify: (3 + 2i)(4 - i)

Solution: (3 + 2i)(4 - i) = 12 - 3i + 8i - 2i² = 12 + 5i - 2(-1) = 12 + 5i + 2 = 14 + 5i

2. Find the conjugate of (5 - 7i) and verify that their product is real.

Solution: Conjugate = 5 + 7i. Product = (5 - 7i)(5 + 7i) = 25 + 35i - 35i - 49i² = 25 - 49(-1) = 25 + 49 = 74 (real number)

3. Identify the property: 8 × (9 + 2) = 8 × 9 + 8 × 2

Solution: Distributive property of multiplication over addition.

4. Divide: (6 + 8i) ÷ (2 + i)

Solution: (6 + 8i)/(2 + i) = [(6 + 8i)(2 - i)]/[(2 + i)(2 - i)] = [12 - 6i + 16i - 8i²]/[4 - i²] = [12 + 10i + 8]/[4 + 1] = (20 + 10i)/5 = 4 + 2i

5. Classify the following numbers as rational or irrational: √16, √5, 0.75, π

Solution: √16 = 4 (rational), √5 (irrational), 0.75 = 3/4 (rational), π (irrational)

6. Simplify: i³ + i⁴ + i⁵

Solution: i³ = -i, i⁴ = 1, i⁵ = i. Sum = -i + 1 + i = 1

7. Find the multiplicative inverse of 3 - 4i

Solution: 1/(3 - 4i) = (3 + 4i)/[(3 - 4i)(3 + 4i)] = (3 + 4i)/(9 + 16) = (3 + 4i)/25 = 3/25 + (4/25)i

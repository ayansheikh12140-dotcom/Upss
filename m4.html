Chapter 4: Factorization

Comprehensive Notes, Questions, and Practice for Sindh Textbook Board

Chapter Notes

This chapter covers various methods of factorizing algebraic expressions and their applications.

What is Factorization?

Definition

Factorization is the process of expressing an algebraic expression as a product of its factors.

If an expression E = F × G, then F and G are called factors of E.

Examples

6x + 9 = 3(2x + 3) → Factors: 3 and (2x + 3)

x² - 4 = (x - 2)(x + 2) → Factors: (x - 2) and (x + 2)

x² + 5x + 6 = (x + 2)(x + 3) → Factors: (x + 2) and (x + 3)

Methods of Factorization

1. Taking Common Factors

Identify and take out the common factor from all terms.

Example: 6x² + 9x = 3x(2x + 3)

Example: 4a²b + 6ab² = 2ab(2a + 3b)

2. Grouping of Terms

Group terms with common factors and then factorize each group.

Example: ax + ay + bx + by = a(x + y) + b(x + y) = (a + b)(x + y)

Example: x² + 3x + 2x + 6 = x(x + 3) + 2(x + 3) = (x + 2)(x + 3)

3. Using Algebraic Identities

Apply standard algebraic identities to factorize expressions.

  • a² - b² = (a - b)(a + b)
  • a² + 2ab + b² = (a + b)²
  • a² - 2ab + b² = (a - b)²
  • a³ + b³ = (a + b)(a² - ab + b²)
  • a³ - b³ = (a - b)(a² + ab + b²)
  • a³ + b³ + c³ - 3abc = (a + b + c)(a² + b² + c² - ab - bc - ca)

Example: x² - 9 = (x)² - (3)² = (x - 3)(x + 3)

Example: 4x² + 12x + 9 = (2x)² + 2(2x)(3) + (3)² = (2x + 3)²

4. Splitting the Middle Term (for Quadratic Expressions)

For expressions of the form ax² + bx + c, find two numbers whose product is ac and sum is b.

Example: x² + 5x + 6

Find two numbers that multiply to 6 and add to 5: 2 and 3

x² + 2x + 3x + 6 = x(x + 2) + 3(x + 2) = (x + 2)(x + 3)

5. Perfect Square Trinomials

Recognize and factorize expressions that are perfect squares.

Example: x² + 6x + 9 = (x + 3)²

Example: 4x² - 12x + 9 = (2x - 3)²

Special Cases of Factorization

Factorization of Sum and Difference of Cubes

x³ + 8 = x³ + 2³ = (x + 2)(x² - 2x + 4)

27x³ - 1 = (3x)³ - 1³ = (3x - 1)(9x² + 3x + 1)

Factorization of Expressions with Higher Powers

x⁴ - 16 = (x²)² - 4² = (x² - 4)(x² + 4) = (x - 2)(x + 2)(x² + 4)

x⁶ - 64 = (x³)² - 8² = (x³ - 8)(x³ + 8)

Applications of Factorization

  • Simplifying algebraic expressions
  • Solving quadratic equations
  • Finding LCM and HCF of algebraic expressions
  • Simplifying rational expressions
  • Solving word problems

Multiple Choice Questions (MCQs)

1. What are the factors of x² - 9?

a) (x-3)²
b) (x+3)²
c) (x-3)(x+3)
d) (x-9)(x+1)

Answer: c) (x-3)(x+3) (using a² - b² formula)

2. Factorize: 6x² + 9x

a) 3x(2x + 3)
b) 3(2x² + 3x)
c) 2x(3x + 9)
d) x(6x + 9)

Answer: a) 3x(2x + 3) (taking common factor)

3. Which method is used for x² + 5x + 6?

a) Common factor
b) Grouping
c) Splitting middle term
d) Using identities

Answer: c) Splitting middle term (find numbers that multiply to 6 and add to 5)

4. Factorize: x³ + 8

a) (x+2)³
b) (x+2)(x²-2x+4)
c) (x+2)(x²+2x+4)
d) (x+4)(x²-4x+4)

Answer: b) (x+2)(x²-2x+4) (using a³ + b³ formula)

5. What is the common factor in 4a²b + 6ab²?

a) 2ab
b) 4ab
c) 2a²b
d) 6ab²

Answer: a) 2ab (greatest common factor)

6. Factorize: x² + 8x + 16

a) (x+4)²
b) (x+8)²
c) (x+2)(x+8)
d) (x+4)(x+4)

Answer: a) (x+4)² (perfect square trinomial)

7. Which is a factor of x² - 5x + 6?

a) x-1
b) x-2
c) x-4
d) x-6

Answer: b) x-2 (since (x-2)(x-3) = x² - 5x + 6)

8. Factorize: 9x² - 25

a) (3x-5)²
b) (3x+5)²
c) (3x-5)(3x+5)
d) (9x-5)(x+5)

Answer: c) (3x-5)(3x+5) (using a² - b² formula)

9. What method is used for ax + ay + bx + by?

a) Common factor
b) Grouping
c) Splitting middle term
d) Using identities

Answer: b) Grouping (group terms with common factors)

10. Factorize: x⁴ - 16

a) (x²-4)²
b) (x²-4)(x²+4)
c) (x-2)⁴
d) (x-4)(x³+4)

Answer: b) (x²-4)(x²+4) (using a² - b² formula twice)

Short Questions

  1. Define factorization.
    Answer: Expressing an algebraic expression as a product of its factors.
  2. What is the common factor in 12x² + 18x?
    Answer: 6x (greatest common factor).
  3. Factorize: x² - 25
    Answer: (x-5)(x+5) using a² - b² formula.
  4. What method is used for x² + 7x + 12?
    Answer: Splitting the middle term.
  5. Factorize: 4x² - 9
    Answer: (2x-3)(2x+3) using a² - b² formula.
  6. What are the factors of x³ - 8?
    Answer: (x-2)(x²+2x+4) using a³ - b³ formula.
  7. Factorize by grouping: ax + bx + ay + by
    Answer: (a+b)(x+y).
  8. Is x² + 4x + 4 a perfect square?
    Answer: Yes, (x+2)².
  9. Factorize: 2x² + 8x + 6
    Answer: 2(x² + 4x + 3) = 2(x+1)(x+3).
  10. What is the relationship between factors and roots?
    Answer: If (x-a) is a factor, then x=a is a root of the equation.

Long Questions

  1. Explain different methods of factorization with examples.
    Answer: 1) Common factor: 6x+9=3(2x+3). 2) Grouping: ax+ay+bx+by=a(x+y)+b(x+y)=(a+b)(x+y). 3) Using identities: x²-9=(x-3)(x+3). 4) Splitting middle term: x²+5x+6=x²+2x+3x+6=x(x+2)+3(x+2)=(x+2)(x+3). 5) Perfect squares: x²+6x+9=(x+3)².
  2. Describe the process of factorization by splitting the middle term.
    Answer: For ax²+bx+c, find two numbers whose product is a×c and sum is b. Split the middle term using these numbers. Group the terms and factorize each group. Example: x²+5x+6. Product=6, sum=5. Numbers: 2 and 3. x²+2x+3x+6=x(x+2)+3(x+2)=(x+2)(x+3).
  3. Prove the algebraic identity a³ + b³ = (a+b)(a² - ab + b²).
    Answer: RHS = (a+b)(a² - ab + b²) = a(a² - ab + b²) + b(a² - ab + b²) = a³ - a²b + ab² + a²b - ab² + b³ = a³ + b³ = LHS. Hence proved.
  4. Explain factorization of perfect square trinomials with examples.
    Answer: A perfect square trinomial is of the form a² ± 2ab + b² = (a ± b)². Examples: x²+6x+9=(x)²+2(x)(3)+(3)²=(x+3)². 4x²-12x+9=(2x)²-2(2x)(3)+(3)²=(2x-3)². To recognize: first and last terms must be perfect squares, middle term must be twice the product of square roots.
  5. Discuss the applications of factorization in solving real-world problems.
    Answer: Factorization is used in: 1) Solving equations (quadratic equations), 2) Simplifying algebraic fractions, 3) Finding dimensions from area expressions, 4) Optimization problems, 5) Physics and engineering calculations, 6) Computer algorithms, 7) Cryptography. Example: Finding rectangle dimensions when area is given as x²+5x+6, factors give possible length and width.

1. Factorize: 12x² + 18x

Solution: 12x² + 18x = 6x(2x + 3) (taking common factor)

2. Factorize: x² - 7x + 12

Solution: x² - 7x + 12 = x² - 3x - 4x + 12 = x(x - 3) - 4(x - 3) = (x - 3)(x - 4)

3. Factorize: 16x² - 25

Solution: 16x² - 25 = (4x)² - (5)² = (4x - 5)(4x + 5) (using a² - b² formula)

4. Factorize: x³ - 27

Solution: x³ - 27 = x³ - 3³ = (x - 3)(x² + 3x + 9) (using a³ - b³ formula)

5. Factorize: 2x² + 11x + 12

Solution: 2x² + 11x + 12 = 2x² + 8x + 3x + 12 = 2x(x + 4) + 3(x + 4) = (2x + 3)(x + 4)

6. Factorize: x⁴ - 81

Solution: x⁴ - 81 = (x²)² - (9)² = (x² - 9)(x² + 9) = (x - 3)(x + 3)(x² + 9)

7. Factorize: 3ax + 3ay + 2bx + 2by

Solution: 3ax + 3ay + 2bx + 2by = 3a(x + y) + 2b(x + y) = (3a + 2b)(x + y)

8. Factorize: 9x² + 24x + 16

Solution: 9x² + 24x + 16 = (3x)² + 2(3x)(4) + (4)² = (3x + 4)² (perfect square)

Chapter 2: Shah Abdul Latif Bhitai üïå

A Study Guide on the Great Sufi Poet of Sindh

Chapter Notes üìú

This chapter introduces students to **Shah Abdul Latif Bhitai**, one of the greatest Sufi poets of the Sindhi language and a national hero of Sindh.

Life and Background

  • **Full Name:** Shah Abdul Latif.
  • **Title:** He is famously known as **Bhitai** because he settled on a sandy hill or **"Bhitt"** (mound) near the banks of the Kolo River (now an old bed of the Indus River).
  • **Period:** He lived during the **18th Century** (born c. 1689 ‚Äì died 1752 AD).
  • **Nature:** He was a **Sufi saint** and traveler who devoted his life to poetry, music, and the simple life.

Contribution to Literature and Music

His masterpiece is the **Shah Jo Risalo**.

  • **Shah Jo Risalo:** The compilation of his poetry, containing his unique style called **Wai** (or Bayt).
  • **Themes:** His poetry is rich in local folk tales, Sufi philosophy, love (both earthly and Divine), local culture, and the beauty of nature.
  • **Sufi Love:** The love stories (e.g., Sassi Punnu, Sohni Mahiwal) are used as **allegories** for the soul's journey (seeker) toward God (the beloved).
  • **Musical Instrument:** He invented a simple musical instrument called the **Tamburo** (a modified string instrument) to accompany his verses.

Shrine and Annual Urs

  • **Shrine:** His tomb is located in the village of **Bhit Shah**, Sindh.
  • **Urs:** An annual festival is held at his shrine to commemorate his death anniversary, where his poetry is sung and celebrated.

Short Questions ‚ùì

  1. **Who was Shah Abdul Latif Bhitai?**
    Answer: He was a famous Sufi poet and saint of the 18th century, considered the greatest exponent of Sindhi language and culture.
  2. **What is the meaning of 'Bhittai'?**
    Answer: 'Bhittai' means 'the dweller of the sandy mound' (Bhitt), referring to the place where he settled and passed away.
  3. **Name the main collection of his poetry.**
    Answer: The main collection of his poetry is the **Shah Jo Risalo**.
  4. **What is the central theme of his poetry?**
    Answer: The central theme is **Divine love** (Sufism), often expressed through the simple lives and struggles of local people and folk heroines.
  5. **Why is the Tamburo important?**
    Answer: The Tamburo is a simple string instrument that Shah Abdul Latif is credited with modifying or inventing to accompany the singing of his verses.

Long Questions üìö

  1. **Describe the life and characteristics of Shah Abdul Latif Bhitai.**
    Answer: Shah Abdul Latif (c. 1689‚Äì1752) was a Sufi dervish who lived a simple, wandering life. He was a poet, a musician, and a scholar. His characteristics include his deep piety, humility, keen observation of nature and local life, and his ability to transform local folk tales into powerful spiritual allegories.
  2. **Explain the importance of 'Shah Jo Risalo' in Sindhi literature.**
    Answer: The Shah Jo Risalo is the foundational work of classical Sindhi literature. It is significant because it standardized the Sindhi language of his time, introduced a unique lyrical style (**Wai**), and preserved Sindhi culture and tradition by intertwining them with universal Sufi concepts of love, struggle, and devotion.
  3. **How does Shah Abdul Latif use local folk tales in his poetry?**
    Answer: He uses local folk tales (like Sassui-Punhun, Sohni-Mahiwal, Marui-Omer) not just as stories, but as spiritual parables (**allegories**). The separation of the heroine from her beloved represents the **human soul's separation from God**, and her relentless journey represents the **seeker's struggle** to achieve union with the Divine.

Chapter 2: Kinematics üèÉ

Study of Motion Without Considering its Cause

Core Concepts ‚öôÔ∏è

**Kinematics** is the branch of mechanics that deals with the study of motion of objects **without** reference to the forces which cause the motion.

1. Rest and Motion

  • **Rest:** An object is at rest if it **does not change** its position with respect to its surroundings.
  • **Motion:** An object is in motion if it **changes** its position with respect to its surroundings.
  • **Key Fact:** Rest and motion are **relative** terms.

2. Scalars and Vectors

  • **Scalar:** A quantity described completely by **magnitude only** (e.g., Distance, Speed, Mass, Time).
  • **Vector:** A quantity described completely by **magnitude AND direction** (e.g., Displacement, Velocity, Acceleration, Force).

3. Displacement and Velocity

  • **Distance:** The actual path length covered by a body (Scalar).
  • **Displacement ($\vec{d}$):** The shortest straight-line distance between the initial and final positions (Vector).
  • **Speed ($v$):** Rate of change of distance (Scalar). Formula: $v = \frac{\text{Distance}}{\text{Time}}$. Unit: $\text{m/s}$.
  • **Velocity ($\vec{v}$):** Rate of change of displacement (Vector). Formula: $\vec{v} = \frac{\text{Displacement}}{\text{Time}}$. Unit: $\text{m/s}$.
  • **Uniform Velocity:** Velocity remains constant (same magnitude and direction).
  • **Average Velocity:** $\text{Average } \vec{v} = \frac{\text{Total Displacement}}{\text{Total Time}}$.

4. Acceleration

  • **Acceleration ($\vec{a}$):** Rate of change of velocity (Vector). Formula: $$\vec{a} = \frac{\text{Change in Velocity}}{\text{Time}} = \frac{\vec{v}_f - \vec{v}_i}{t}$$ Unit: $\text{m/s}^2$.
  • **Uniform Acceleration:** The rate of change of velocity is constant.
  • **Deceleration (Negative Acceleration):** When the velocity of a body decreases.
  • **Gravity:** When an object moves freely under gravity, its acceleration is $g \approx 9.8 \text{ m/s}^2$ (downwards).

Equations of Uniformly Accelerated Motion

These equations are valid only when acceleration ($\vec{a}$) is **constant**.

Equation Formula
**First Equation**$$v_f = v_i + at$$
**Second Equation**$$S = v_i t + \frac{1}{2} a t^2$$
**Third Equation**$$2aS = v_f^2 - v_i^2$$

Where:

  • $v_i$ = Initial Velocity
  • $v_f$ = Final Velocity
  • $a$ = Uniform Acceleration
  • $t$ = Time
  • $S$ = Distance/Displacement Covered

Short Questions ‚ùì

  1. **Define Kinematics.**
    Answer: Kinematics is the study of motion of objects without considering the cause (forces) of the motion.
  2. **Differentiate between distance and displacement.**
    Answer: **Distance** is the total path covered (scalar), while **displacement** is the shortest distance from the initial to the final point (vector).
  3. **When is acceleration considered uniform?**
    Answer: Acceleration is uniform when the velocity of the body changes by **equal amounts in equal intervals of time**.
  4. **Give an example of negative acceleration (deceleration).**
    Answer: When a driver applies brakes to slow down a moving car, the car's acceleration is in the direction opposite to its velocity.
  5. **What is the value of 'g' and what does it represent?**
    Answer: The value of $g$ (acceleration due to gravity) is approximately $9.8 \text{ m/s}^2$. It represents the constant acceleration experienced by any object falling freely near the Earth's surface.

Long Questions üìö

  1. **Derive the first equation of motion ($v_f = v_i + at$).**
    Answer: Acceleration is defined as the rate of change of velocity: $$a = \frac{\text{Change in Velocity}}{\text{Time}} = \frac{v_f - v_i}{t}$$ Rearranging the terms to solve for $v_f$: $$at = v_f - v_i$$ $$\Rightarrow v_f = v_i + at$$
  2. **Derive the second equation of motion ($S = v_i t + \frac{1}{2} a t^2$).**
    Answer: The distance $S$ covered is given by the product of average velocity and time. For uniform acceleration, the average velocity is: $$\text{Average Velocity} = \frac{v_i + v_f}{2}$$ Distance $S$ is: $$S = (\text{Average Velocity}) \times t = \left(\frac{v_i + v_f}{2}\right) t$$ Substitute $v_f$ from the first equation ($v_f = v_i + at$): $$S = \left(\frac{v_i + (v_i + at)}{2}\right) t = \left(\frac{2v_i + at}{2}\right) t$$ $$S = \left(v_i + \frac{1}{2} at\right) t$$ $$\Rightarrow S = v_i t + \frac{1}{2} a t^2$$
  3. **Differentiate between Speed and Velocity with respect to their magnitude and direction.**
    Answer: **Speed** is a **scalar** quantity that only requires magnitude (e.g., $50 \text{ km/h}$) and gives the rate of distance covered. **Velocity** is a **vector** quantity that requires both magnitude and direction (e.g., $50 \text{ km/h}$ East) and gives the rate of displacement. The magnitude of velocity is speed, but velocity must specify direction.

Numerical Problems üî¢

Practice applying the equations of motion.

1. A car accelerates from rest ( $0 \text{ m/s}$) at $2 \text{ m/s}^2$ for $5 \text{ s}$. Find its final velocity.

Given: $v_i = 0 \text{ m/s}$, $a = 2 \text{ m/s}^2$, $t = 5 \text{ s}$. Using $v_f = v_i + at$: $$v_f = 0 + (2 \text{ m/s}^2)(5 \text{ s})$$ $$v_f = 10 \text{ m/s}$$

2. A motorcycle moving at $15 \text{ m/s}$ accelerates at $3 \text{ m/s}^2$ over a distance of $50 \text{ m}$. Find its final velocity.

Given: $v_i = 15 \text{ m/s}$, $a = 3 \text{ m/s}^2$, $S = 50 \text{ m}$. Using $2aS = v_f^2 - v_i^2$: $$2(3 \text{ m/s}^2)(50 \text{ m}) = v_f^2 - (15 \text{ m/s})^2$$ $$300 = v_f^2 - 225$$ $$v_f^2 = 300 + 225 = 525$$ $$v_f = \sqrt{525} \approx 22.91 \text{ m/s}$$

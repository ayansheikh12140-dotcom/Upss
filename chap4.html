Chapter 4: Chemical Bonding

Comprehensive Notes, Questions, and Practice for Sindh Textbook Board

Chapter Notes

This chapter discusses how atoms combine to form molecules through chemical bonds, including ionic and covalent bonds.

Types of Chemical Bonds

  • Ionic Bond: Transfer of electrons from metal to non-metal, forming ions (e.g., NaCl).
  • Covalent Bond: Sharing of electrons between non-metals (e.g., H₂, CO₂).

Properties of Ionic Compounds

  • High melting/boiling points
  • Conduct electricity in molten state
  • Soluble in water

Properties of Covalent Compounds

  • Low melting/boiling points
  • Do not conduct electricity
  • Insoluble in water

Multiple Choice Questions (MCQs)

1. Which bond involves sharing of electrons?

a) Ionic
b) Covalent
c) Metallic
d) Hydrogen

Answer: b) Covalent

2. Ionic bonds form between:

a) Two metals
b) Two non-metals
c) Metal and non-metal
d) Gases

Answer: c) Metal and non-metal

3. Which compound has covalent bonds?

a) NaCl
b) MgO
c) H₂O
d) CaCl₂

Answer: c) H₂O

4. Ionic compounds conduct electricity in:

a) Solid state
b) Molten state
c) Gaseous state
d) Never

Answer: b) Molten state

5. Lewis dot structure shows:

a) Atomic mass
b) Valence electrons
c) Protons
d) Neutrons

Answer: b) Valence electrons

Short Questions

  1. Define chemical bonding.
    Answer: The force that holds atoms together in a molecule.
  2. What is an ionic bond?
    Answer: Bond formed by transfer of electrons.
  3. What is a covalent bond?
    Answer: Bond formed by sharing of electrons.
  4. Give an example of ionic compound.
    Answer: Sodium chloride (NaCl).
  5. Give an example of covalent compound.
    Answer: Water (H₂O).
  6. Why do ionic compounds have high melting points?
    Answer: Strong electrostatic forces between ions.
  7. Do covalent compounds conduct electricity?
    Answer: No, they are insulators.
  8. What is a Lewis dot structure?
    Answer: Diagram showing valence electrons as dots.
  9. Differentiate between ionic and covalent bonds.
    Answer: Ionic: transfer; Covalent: sharing.
  10. What are valence electrons?
    Answer: Electrons in the outermost shell.

Long Questions

  1. Explain the formation of ionic bond with example.
    Answer: Metal loses electrons to form cation, non-metal gains to form anion, e.g., Na + Cl → NaCl.
  2. Describe covalent bonding with example.
    Answer: Non-metals share electrons, e.g., H-H in H₂.
  3. Compare properties of ionic and covalent compounds.
    Answer: Ionic: high MP, conduct in molten, soluble; Covalent: low MP, insulators, insoluble.
  4. What is octet rule? Explain with example.
    Answer: Atoms tend to have 8 electrons in valence shell, e.g., NaCl formation.
  5. Draw Lewis dot structures for H₂ and HCl.
    Answer: H:H for H₂; H:Cl for HCl.

Numerical Problems

Practice these numerical problems based on Chapter 4 concepts. Click "Show Solution" to reveal the answer.

1. How many valence electrons does sodium have?

Solution: Sodium (Na) has 1 valence electron.

2. Determine the type of bond in MgO.

Solution: Ionic bond (metal and non-metal).

3. How many electrons are shared in a single covalent bond?

Solution: 2 electrons (one pair).

4. Write the Lewis dot structure for chlorine (Cl₂).

Solution: :Cl:Cl: (each Cl has 7 electrons, sharing one pair).

5. Calculate the number of bonds in CO₂.

Solution: 2 double bonds (C=O).

6. What is the formula for magnesium chloride?

Solution: MgCl₂ (ionic).

7. How many valence electrons does carbon have?

Solution: 4 valence electrons.

8. Identify the bond type in NH₃.

Solution: Covalent bonds.

9. Draw Lewis structure for O₂.

Solution: :O::O: (double bond).

10. What is the charge on sodium ion in NaCl?

Solution: +1.
